**Instructional Outline for Communicating Antigravity (Product Perspective)**

Use this outline as a clear, non-technical communication framework when describing Antigravity to users, partners, professionals, or stakeholders. The goal is to position the product as an empowering, human-centred tool rather than a technical system. Speak from the perspective of lived experience, wellbeing, and practical support. Structure every presentation, demo, or document around these four layers, keeping language warm, accessible, and focused on people first.

1. **Start with the Human Purpose (Why Antigravity Exists)**  
   Open by framing the core problem and vision:  
   “Most digital records today are fragmented and clinical — they only capture slices of a person’s health or situation. Antigravity is different. It is a personal welfare vault that holds the full, rich context of someone’s life so they can be supported as a whole human being, not just a set of symptoms or data points.  
   It recognises that people are socially interdependent — we thrive through relationships with family, carers, partners, doctors, mental health professionals, social workers, legal advocates, and others. Antigravity gives individuals secure control over their own information while making it easier to share the right context with the right people at the right time, always with explicit permission.”

2. **Explain the Philosophical Foundations (What Antigravity Stands For)**  
   Present the underlying beliefs simply and relationally:  
   - Human-centric: Every design decision begins with the person’s lived reality, dignity, and right to be understood in context.  
   - Interdependence over isolation: We are not alone; wellbeing emerges through safe, trusted connections with others who can help.  
   - Holistic welfare: True support spans physical health, safety, belonging, esteem, personal growth, and rights — not just medical facts.  
   - Empowerment through context: Providing richer, layered personal context helps professionals and loved ones advocate more effectively and compassionately, whether in healthcare, housing, legal matters, mental health, or everyday life events.  
   - Respect for complexity: No single model captures every human experience, so the vault is built to be flexible and inclusive of different ways of understanding needs and situations.  
   Emphasise that this philosophy moves beyond traditional “digital health records” to become a tool for human flourishing, human rights realisation, and collaborative care.

3. **Describe the Product Framework (How Antigravity Works from a User View)**  
   Describe the experience and value without technical terms:  
   - It acts as a secure, personal vault where individuals organise and store information about their own welfare and life context.  
   - Information is thoughtfully layered so it can be viewed through helpful lenses — for example, starting with a well-known model of human needs that progresses from basic survival and safety up through relationships, self-worth, and personal growth.  
   - Users decide exactly who sees what and when: a doctor might receive medical and safety context; a social worker might see housing and support network details; a family member or partner might receive relational and emotional context. Permissions are clear, revocable, and purpose-specific.  
   - The vault supports different life situations — from everyday wellbeing to complex challenges such as mental health episodes, disability support, housing instability, or major life events — by allowing the same information to be presented in multiple meaningful ways.  
   - The result is better-coordinated support, fewer repeated explanations, stronger advocacy, and a greater sense of being seen and respected as a whole person.

4. **Close with the User Benefit and Invitation (Call to Action)**  
   End on empowerment and possibility:  
   “Antigravity puts the person back in the centre. It respects interdependence, protects privacy, and creates the conditions for better human connections and outcomes. Whether you are the individual, a caring professional, or a family member, the vault makes it easier to work together with dignity and context.  
   We are still in the exploratory phase — building, testing, and refining with real human needs in mind before formalising standards. Your feedback and use cases will shape how it grows.”

**Delivery Tips**  
- Use stories or simple scenarios (e.g., “Imagine a person experiencing homelessness who needs to share housing context with a social worker while also giving their GP medical background — without handing over everything”).  
- Avoid jargon; translate every concept into benefits for real people.  
- Keep slides or documents visual and minimal — focus on layered needs diagrams (high-level only) and trust/permission icons.  
- Always tie back to human-centric informatics as the broader movement this product serves.

**Technical Definition (for internal/project documentation only)**

Antigravity, the product expression of the Personal Welfare Informatics Vault (PWIV), is intended to be built on open, standards-based technologies that enable flexible, interoperable, and validated data representations. It will use RDF and SHACL (among other compatible tools) to support multiple overlapping models of human needs and welfare evaluation.  

Maslow’s hierarchy serves as the initial representation and evaluation model, providing a clear layered structure that can be applied to the stored information. Other models, frameworks, and evaluation approaches may coexist or be added over time. These could include representations based on clinical assessment forms, standardised mental health checklists used for diagnostic support in psychiatry, eligibility criteria such as those in the NDIS (National Disability Insurance Scheme), actuarial or legal evaluations of the cost or impact of specific life events, and any additional culturally, clinically, or contextually relevant frameworks that emerge through research and user needs.  

This multi-model capability allows the same underlying personal data to be viewed, validated, and shared through whichever lens is most helpful for a given situation or professional context, while the individual retains control over their vault. All such representations remain exploratory at this stage, with the intention of producing standardised, reusable components through iterative research before advancing to formal Respec-style specifications.
