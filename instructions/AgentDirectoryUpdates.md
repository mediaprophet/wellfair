# Draft Definition File: Agency & Guardianship Implementation (The Social Book)

**Application:** wellfare 
**Module:** The Social Book (Agency & Guardianship Relations)
**Purpose:** To define the implementation instructions for extending the application into a networked ecosystem of delegated authority, supporting complex, domain-specific guardianship and agency relations.

---

## 1. Core Architectural Paradigm: Selfhood vs. Personhood

The underlying ontology must mathematically separate the user's private reality from their social delegations.

*   **Selfhood (The Vault / Sanctuary Mode):** This domain pertains to aspects of the self that have no meaningful or just requirement to be shared with anyone else[cite: 2].
*   **Personhood (The Agency Matrix):** This domain represents the relationship between a person and both socioeconomics and law, which is commonly referred to as agency[cite: 2]. All guardianship relations are mapped and executed within this layer.

---

## 2. The "Social Book" (Agent Management Engine)

The system utilizes a "Social Book," functioning as a highly secure, permissions-based address book[cite: 2]. It is designed to list contacts and assert permissions and roles on a mutually agreed basis[cite: 2].

### 2.1 Verifiable Role Credentials
Implementation requires strict access control based on qualified, verifiable identity and expertise.

*   **Agent/Principal Structure:** The software must utilize an agent/principal agent structure where several agents can be associated with a principal to serve particular needs[cite: 1, 2].
*   **Credential Ingestion:** When a professional is added to the Social Book, their professional qualifications and certifications must be processed as verifiable claims or credentials[cite: 2].
*   **Derivative Key Generation:** These verified credentials act as the cryptographic foundation to generate derivative keys, linking the agent to specific, accepted guardianship roles within their field of expertise[cite: 2].

### 2.2 Shared Values Agreements
*   **Value(s) Credentials:** The connection process must employ "value(s) credentials" to define foundational expectations for how agents treat one another[cite: 2].
*   **Standardized Mapping:** These credentials should be mapped to well-known instruments, such as the United Nations Human Rights Legal Instruments, providing a mechanism to assess if agreements are breached[cite: 2].

---

## 3. Domains of Delegated Authority

Guardianship must be implemented as a spectrum of delegated authority rather than a single, blanket legal status[cite: 1]. The system must support role assignment across distinct domains:

*   **Traditional Care Domains:** Decision-making for Medical/Healthcare, Financial asset management, Legal representation, and Social or Personal Welfare[cite: 1].
*   **Supervisory / Protective Domains:** Oversight mechanisms used to prevent abuse, neglect, or self-harm[cite: 1].
*   **Emerging Digital Domains:** Management of Digital Identity, Data Privacy and Consent, and Reputational management[cite: 1].
*   **AI Proxy Guardianship:** The oversight of software autonomous agents acting on behalf of the principal user[cite: 1].

---

## 4. Accountability, Auditing, and Revocation

To protect the principal user, the system must enforce strict accountability for all delegated agents.

*   **Symmetrical Responsibilities:** Expectations, responsibilities, and permissions must not be asymmetrical[cite: 1, 2].
*   **The Evidence Chain:** The system must maintain an immutable evidence chain to help distinguish malicious or negligent behavior from honest mistakes made by guardians[cite: 2].
*   **Insured Actions:** By explicitly linking specific roles to verified professional credentials, the app ensures that agents are only casting judgments within their insured specializations, meaning mistakes can be covered by insurance systems[cite: 2].
*   **Revocation Mechanics (Implementation Requirement):** The UI/UX and underlying smart contracts/DIDs must support immediate, unilateral revocation of an agent's derivative keys if a shared values agreement is breached or the relationship turns adversarial.

---

## 5. Contingency Protocols: The "Digital Good Samaritan"

For individuals lacking reliable human contacts, the system must provide autonomous failsafes.

*   **Next of Kin AI:** The system must feature an AI-empowered decision support system that can analyze individual circumstances and intentions to provide informed recommendations in emergencies, acting as a dependable resource in an auditably protected manner[cite: 2].
*   **The Digital Will & Deadman Switch:** The app must support a "Digital Will" bot to address posthumous needs[cite: 2].
*   **Executor Actions:** This includes managing the assignment of assets, executing digital executor instructions (such as account passwords or bodily disposal methods), and triggering a deadman switch to publish or delete digital records as previously defined by the user[cite: 2].
